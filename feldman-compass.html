Privacy Policy

Feldman Compass  •  Effective Date: 18/12/2025  •  Developer: Feldman

Introduction

Feldman (“we,” “our,” or “us”) operates the Feldman Compass mobile application (the “App”). This Privacy Policy explains how information is handled when you use the App.

Feldman Compass is designed to function without collecting or storing personal data beyond what is strictly necessary to deliver accurate compass functionality.

Information We Collect

Location Information

The App requests location access solely to determine device orientation and provide accurate compass readings.

  • Used only while the App is open and active
  • Processed entirely on your device
  • Never stored, transmitted, logged, or shared

The App does not access location data in the background.

No Other Data Collection

Feldman Compass does not collect, store, or process:

  • Personal identifiers (such as name, email address, or phone number)
  • User accounts or login credentials
  • Analytics or usage metrics
  • Device identifiers
  • Crash reports tied to individuals

Advertising

The App does not display advertisements and does not integrate with any advertising networks or monetization platforms.

Third-Party Services

Feldman Compass does not use third-party SDKs, analytics tools, or external services that collect user data.

Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, share, or disclose user data to third parties. Because no personal data is collected, there is no data available for sharing.

Data Security

All processing related to compass and location functionality occurs locally on your device. Since no personal data is retained, risks associated with data storage or data breaches are minimized.

Children’s Privacy

Feldman Compass does not knowingly collect personal information from children or users of any age. The App is suitable for all audiences.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated if the App’s functionality or data practices change. Any revisions will be reflected by an updated effective date.
